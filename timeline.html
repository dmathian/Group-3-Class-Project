The Timeline

Timeline of Video Game Graphics Evolution

Trace the history of video game graphics, from the early pixelated graphics to today's hyper-realistic visuals.

1970s

Early Pixel Art

Games like "Pong" and "Space Invaders" set the foundation with simple, pixelated graphics.

1980s

Introduction of Color

Games began to feature colorful graphics, with titles like "Super Mario Bros." pushing the boundaries of 8-bit art.

1990s

3D Graphics Emerge

Games like "Doom" and "Quake" introduced early 3D graphics, revolutionizing video game visuals.

2000s

HD Graphics

With consoles like the Xbox and PlayStation 2, high-definition graphics became the norm.

2010s

Realistic 3D Graphics

Advanced graphics engines enabled highly realistic visuals, with games like "The Last of Us" showcasing lifelike details.

2020s

Ray Tracing and Photorealism

Ray tracing technology and powerful graphics cards allow for near-photorealistic graphics in modern games.
